# Social Media Content Strategy

## Purpose

Create social media content that builds audience, drives engagement, and supports business goals. This skill covers strategy (what to post and why), execution (how to write for each platform), and measurement (what's working and what to change).

## Step 1: Gather Context

Before building a social content plan, clarify:

1. **Business type:** B2B, B2C, DTC, SaaS, services, creator?
2. **Primary goal:** Brand awareness, lead generation, community building, traffic, sales?
3. **Target audience:** Who are they, what do they care about, where do they spend time?
4. **Active platforms:** Which platforms are you currently on? Which should you prioritize?
5. **Resources:** Who creates content? How much time per week? Budget for design or video?
6. **Brand voice:** Formal, casual, witty, authoritative, irreverent? (Provide 3-4 adjectives.)
7. **Current performance:** What's working? What's falling flat? What metrics do you track?

## Step 2: Content Pillars for Social

Social content pillars are not the same as blog content pillars. They're the recurring themes your audience comes to expect from your feed.

### Choose 4-6 Pillars
1. **Educational:** Teach something useful. Tips, frameworks, how-tos, breakdowns.
2. **Behind-the-scenes:** Show the work, the process, the team. Builds trust and relatability.
3. **Social proof:** Customer stories, results, testimonials, case studies.
4. **Industry commentary:** Hot takes, trend analysis, reactions to news. Shows you're paying attention.
5. **Engagement / conversation:** Polls, questions, debates, "agree or disagree?" posts. Drives comments.
6. **Product / company:** Updates, launches, features. Keep to 20% or less of total output.

### The 80/20 Rule
- 80% of content should provide value (educate, entertain, inspire, or connect).
- 20% of content can promote your product, company, or offer.
- Audiences unfollow accounts that only talk about themselves.

### Pillar Rotation
Rotate through pillars to keep the feed varied. A sample weekly rotation for a 5-post-per-week schedule:
| Day | Pillar |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Educational (tip or framework) |
| Tuesday | Industry commentary (reaction or take) |
| Wednesday | Behind-the-scenes or personal story |
| Thursday | Social proof (customer story or result) |
| Friday | Engagement (poll, question, or debate) |

## Step 3: Platform-Specific Formats

---

### 3A: LinkedIn

**Audience:** Professionals, B2B buyers, decision-makers, job seekers, industry practitioners.

**What works:**
- Personal stories with professional lessons.
- Contrarian takes on industry norms.
- Frameworks and step-by-step breakdowns.
- Career and leadership insights.
- Data-backed observations.

**Post Formats:**

**Text post (short):** 100-300 words. One idea, clearly stated.
```
[Hook line — bold claim, question, or surprising statement]

[2-3 sentences of context or story]

[The insight or lesson]

[Call to engagement — question or takeaway]
```

**Text post (long):** 500-1,000 words. Deeper exploration. Use line breaks aggressively.
```
[Hook — first 2-3 lines visible before "see more"]

[Story or setup — 3-4 short paragraphs]

[Core insight — the payoff]

[Numbered list or framework — 3-7 points]

[Closing thought + engagement question]
```

**Carousel (document post):** 5-12 slides. Each slide = one idea.
- Slide 1: Bold headline (the hook).
- Slides 2-10: One point per slide, large text, minimal design.
- Final slide: Summary + CTA (follow, comment, share, visit link).

**Poll:** Ask a question with 2-4 options. Follow up with a post sharing the results and your analysis.

**LinkedIn-Specific Rules:**
- The first 2-3 lines are critical — they appear before the "see more" fold. Front-load the hook.
- Use line breaks between every 1-2 sentences. Dense paragraphs get scrolled past.
- Avoid external links in the post body — LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound links. Put the link in the first comment instead.
- Post natively. LinkedIn favors content created on the platform over shared links.
- Engage with comments in the first 60 minutes. The algorithm rewards active discussion.
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant hashtags. Place at the bottom. Don't over-tag.

### 3B: Twitter / X

**Audience:** Tech, media, politics, finance, crypto, creators, startup ecosystem.

**What works:**
- Sharp, single-idea tweets.
- Threads that break down complex topics.
- Hot takes and contrarian opinions.
- Curated lists and recommendations.
- Real-time commentary on events and trends.

**Post Formats:**

**Single tweet:** 1-2 sentences. One idea, one punch.
- Make it quotable. Would someone screenshot this?
- Remove every unnecessary word. Twitter rewards density.

**Thread:** 5-15 tweets. A structured argument or breakdown.
```
Tweet 1: [Hook — the promise of the thread]
"I've spent 10 years writing cold emails. Here are 9 rules that
actually get replies:"

Tweet 2-N: [One point per tweet, numbered]
"1. Write like a peer, not a vendor..."

Final tweet: [Summary + CTA]
"TL;DR: [core message]. If this was useful, follow me for more
on [topic]. RT the first tweet to share."
```

**Quote tweet:** Add your take to someone else's tweet. Agreement + expansion, or respectful disagreement + your perspective.

**Twitter/X-Specific Rules:**
- First tweet of a thread is the hook. If it doesn't earn a click on "Show this thread," the rest doesn't matter.
- Use numbers in thread hooks: "7 lessons," "5 mistakes," "3 rules." Numbers signal structure and scannability.
- Tweet threads in the morning (8-10 AM in your target timezone) for maximum reach.
- Engage in replies to big accounts in your niche. Thoughtful replies build visibility.
- Avoid hashtags in tweets — they look spammy on X. Exception: live event hashtags.
- Use images and screenshots to break up long threads. Visual tweets get more engagement.
- Pin your best-performing tweet or most important thread to your profile.

### 3C: Instagram

**Audience:** Consumers, lifestyle brands, DTC, visual industries, younger demographics (18-34 primary).

**What works:**
- High-quality visuals and video.
- Reels (short video, 15-90 seconds).
- Carousels with educational or storytelling content.
- Stories for behind-the-scenes and real-time engagement.
- User-generated content and community features.

**Post Formats:**

**Single image post:** Strong visual + value-driven caption.
- Caption structure: Hook (first line) -> story or insight -> CTA.
- Captions can be long on Instagram. 100-300 words performs well.

**Carousel:** 2-10 slides. Swipeable educational or storytelling content.
- Slide 1: Bold headline or question (the hook).
- Slides 2-9: One idea per slide with clean design.
- Slide 10: CTA — save, share, follow, comment.
- Carousel posts tend to have the highest save rate, which signals quality to the algorithm.

**Reels:** 15-90 seconds. Vertical video.
- Hook in the first 1-2 seconds. If you don't grab attention immediately, they scroll.
- Use trending audio when relevant (but don't force it).
- Add text overlays — many watch without sound.
- Post Reels consistently (3-5 per week) for algorithm favor.

**Stories:** Ephemeral (24-hour) content.
- Use for polls, questions, behind-the-scenes, quick tips, and real-time updates.
- Stories keep you at the top of followers' feeds. Post 3-7 stories per day.
- Use interactive stickers (poll, quiz, question box, slider) to drive engagement.

**Instagram-Specific Rules:**
- Hashtags: Use 5-15 relevant hashtags. Mix popular (100K-1M posts), medium (10K-100K), and niche (1K-10K) tags.
- Put hashtags in the caption or first comment — both work.
- Post when your audience is active (check Instagram Insights for your specific data).
- Write alt text for all images. It's good practice and helps discoverability.
- Engage with comments within the first hour. Reply thoughtfully, not just with a heart emoji.

---

### 3D: TikTok

**Audience:** Gen Z and millennials, but expanding rapidly across all demographics. High engagement, discovery-driven.

**What works:**
- Authentic, unpolished video. Production value is less important than content value.
- Educational content ("learn something in 60 seconds").
- Storytelling with a hook and payoff.
- Trend participation (sounds, formats, challenges) with a brand-relevant twist.
- POV and "day in the life" content.

**Post Formats:**

**Educational TikTok (30-60 seconds):**
```
[Hook — text overlay + spoken: "Here's something most marketers get wrong about..."]
[Setup — 10-15 seconds of context]
[Payoff — the insight, tip, or revelation]
[CTA — "Follow for more" or "Save this for later"]
```

**Storytelling TikTok (30-90 seconds):**
```
[Hook — "The craziest thing happened when we..."]
[Build tension — what went wrong or what was unexpected]
[Resolution — what happened and what you learned]
[CTA or open loop for next video]
```

**Trend participation:**
- Use trending sounds within the first 48 hours of the trend emerging.
- Put your brand or niche spin on the format. Don't copy it exactly.
- If the trend doesn't connect to your content pillars, skip it.

**TikTok-Specific Rules:**
- The first 1-2 seconds decide everything. Hook immediately or lose the viewer.
- Use text overlays — they increase watch time and work for sound-off viewers.
- Post 1-3 times per day when building. Once established, 3-5 per week minimum.
- Engage with comments by creating response videos. The algorithm rewards this.
- Use 3-5 hashtags. Include 1-2 niche-specific and 1-2 broader discovery hashtags.
- TikTok is a discovery platform. Followers matter less than content quality. Any video can go viral regardless of account size.
- Vertical video only (9:16 aspect ratio).
- Repurpose TikToks as Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts (remove the TikTok watermark first — Instagram suppresses watermarked content).

## Step 4: Posting Cadence

### Recommended Minimums
| Platform | Minimum | Ideal for Growth | Format Focus |
|----------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| LinkedIn | 3x/week | 5x/week (weekdays) | Text posts, carousels |
| Twitter/X | 1-2x/day | 3-5x/day | Tweets, threads (1-2/week) |
| Instagram | 3x/week | 5-7x/week + daily Stories | Reels, carousels, Stories |
| TikTok | 3x/week | 1-2x/day | Short-form video |

### Consistency Matters More Than Volume
- Posting 3 times per week consistently for 6 months beats posting daily for 3 weeks then stopping.
- Build a sustainable cadence first. Increase volume only when you have the content pipeline to support it.
- Batch content creation: dedicate 2-3 hours per week to creating the week's content in one sitting.

### Timing
- Use platform analytics to find when your audience is most active.
- General guidelines (adjust based on your data):
  - **LinkedIn:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM or 12-1 PM.
  - **Twitter/X:** Weekdays, 8-10 AM and 5-7 PM.
  - **Instagram:** Weekdays 11 AM-1 PM, evenings 7-9 PM. Weekends for lifestyle content.
  - **TikTok:** Evenings 6-10 PM, weekends. But TikTok's algorithm is less time-sensitive.

## Step 5: Hook Writing

The hook is the first 1-2 lines (text) or first 1-3 seconds (video). It determines whether someone engages or scrolls past.

### Hook Formulas

**The Contrarian:**
"Most [role/people] think [common belief]. They're wrong. Here's why..."

**The Number:**
"I've [done X thing] [number] times. Here are [N] lessons I wish I knew on day 1."

**The Story Opener:**
"Last week, I [did something unexpected/failed at something/discovered something]."

**The Question:**
"Why do most [category] fail at [thing]? It comes down to one mistake."

**The List Promise:**
"[N] [things] that will [desirable outcome] (save this for later)."

**The Proof:**
"We went from [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]. Here's the exact playbook."

**The Curiosity Gap:**
"There's one [strategy/tactic/tool] that [impressive claim]. And almost nobody talks about it."

**The Direct Challenge:**
"Stop [common behavior]. It's killing your [important metric]."

### Hook Rules
1. **Front-load the value.** The hook must promise something specific. "Great tips ahead!" is not a hook.
2. **Create tension.** A hook should open a loop that the reader wants to close.
3. **Be specific.** "How to grow on LinkedIn" is weak. "How I grew from 0 to 50K followers on LinkedIn in 8 months without spending a dollar" is strong.
4. **No throat-clearing.** Don't start with "So," "Well," "I've been thinking lately," or other warm-up phrases. Start at the interesting part.
5. **Test hooks aggressively.** The same content with a different hook can perform 10x differently. When a post underperforms, the hook is usually the problem.

## Step 6: Thread and Carousel Structure

### Thread Structure (Twitter/X and LinkedIn)
```
1. HOOK: Bold claim, surprising stat, or promise.
   Must stand on its own and compel the click.

2-3. CONTEXT: Why this matters. Set the stage.
     Quick background or story.

4-N. CORE CONTENT: The meat. One point per post.
     Use numbered lists for scannability.
     Include examples for each point.

N+1. SUMMARY: Recap the key points in 2-3 sentences.

N+2. CTA: What should the reader do?
     - Follow for more on [topic]
     - Share/RT if useful
     - Save for later
     - Drop your take in the replies
```

### Carousel Structure (LinkedIn and Instagram)
```
Slide 1: HEADLINE
  - Bold, large text
  - One line that communicates the topic and hook
  - "Read this before you..." or "[N] Rules of [Topic]"

Slide 2: CONTEXT (optional)
  - Why this matters
  - Quick framing of the problem

Slides 3-9: ONE POINT PER SLIDE
  - Large heading
  - 1-3 sentences of explanation
  - Keep text readable at mobile size

Slide 10: SUMMARY + CTA
  - Recap or key takeaway
  - "Save this post" / "Follow for more" / "Share with someone who needs this"
```

## Step 7: Hashtag Strategy

### General Rules
- Hashtags help discovery but don't replace great content.
- Use a mix of reach levels: broad (high volume), medium, and niche (low volume, high relevance).
- Research hashtags before using them. Check that they're actively used by your target audience.
- Create a branded hashtag for campaigns or community building — but only if you'll actively promote it.

### Platform-Specific
| Platform | Quantity | Placement |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| LinkedIn | 3-5 | End of post |
| Twitter/X | 0-2 (or none) | Inline or end |
| Instagram | 5-15 | Caption or first comment |
| TikTok | 3-5 | In caption |

### Hashtag Research Process
1. Search your topic on the platform and see what top creators use.
2. Check hashtag volume — too large (10M+) means you'll get buried; too small (under 1K) means no one's looking.
3. Look at the content under the hashtag. Is it your type of content and audience? If the hashtag is full of spam or irrelevant content, skip it.
4. Build a hashtag bank: 20-30 relevant hashtags organized by category. Rotate them to avoid looking repetitive.

## Step 8: Engagement Strategy

### Proactive Engagement
- Spend 15-30 minutes per day engaging with other accounts' content before and after posting.
- Leave thoughtful comments on posts by people in your target audience and industry. Not "Great post!" — add your perspective, a question, or a relevant example.
- Reply to every comment on your posts within the first 2 hours. Early engagement signals quality to algorithms.

### Community Building
- Ask genuine questions in posts. Questions in the last line significantly increase comment rate.
- Respond to DMs. The best relationships on social media happen in private messages.
- Feature and tag others (customers, partners, collaborators). They'll often reshare.
- Create recurring series that people can follow and anticipate (e.g., "Friday frameworks," "Monday metrics").

### Engagement Metrics That Matter
| Metric | What It Means | What to Do |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Impressions | How many people saw the post | Indicates reach. Affected by hook and timing. |
| Engagement rate | (Likes + comments + shares + saves) / impressions | Target 2-5% on LinkedIn, 1-3% on Instagram. |
| Saves | How many people bookmarked the post | High save rate = high-value content. Create more like it. |
| Shares / reposts | How many people shared with their audience | Your best distribution signal. What makes content shareable? |
| Profile visits | How many people clicked to your profile | Indicates curiosity. Optimize your bio and pinned content. |
| Follower growth rate | Net new followers per week/month | Lagging indicator. Focus on content quality and engagement rate. |

### Content Performance Review (Weekly)
1. Identify top 3 posts by engagement rate. What do they have in common? (Topic, format, hook style, time of day)
2. Identify bottom 3 posts. What's different? (Usually: weak hook, wrong format, off-pillar topic, or bad timing)
3. Adjust next week's content plan based on findings.
4. Track trends over 4-8 weeks, not individual posts. One post going viral or flopping is noise. Patterns are signal.

## Output Format

When delivering a social content strategy, include:

1. **Platform recommendation** — which platforms to prioritize and why, based on audience and resources.
2. **Content pillars** — 4-6 pillars with example post ideas for each.
3. **Posting cadence** — weekly schedule with pillar rotation.
4. **Sample content** — 5-10 ready-to-post pieces across recommended platforms.
5. **Hook bank** — 10-15 hooks the user can adapt and reuse.
6. **Hashtag bank** — 20-30 researched hashtags organized by category.
7. **Engagement plan** — daily and weekly engagement activities.
8. **Measurement framework** — what to track, benchmarks, and review cadence.
9. **Assumptions** — what was assumed about audience, goals, or brand voice.
