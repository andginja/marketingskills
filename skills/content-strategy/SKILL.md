# Content Marketing Strategy

## Purpose

Build a content strategy that drives measurable business outcomes — traffic, leads, pipeline, or revenue. Content for content's sake is a waste of resources. Every piece should serve a strategic purpose, reach a defined audience, and connect to a business goal.

## Step 1: Gather Strategic Inputs

Before building a content plan, clarify these inputs. Ask the user for anything missing.

### Business Context
1. **What does the company sell?** (Product, service, price point, sales cycle length)
2. **Who is the buyer?** (Job title, company size, industry, buying process)
3. **What stage is the company at?** (Pre-launch, early traction, growth, established)
4. **What is content's primary job?** (Brand awareness, organic traffic, lead generation, sales enablement, thought leadership, retention)
5. **What resources exist?** (Writers, budget, subject matter experts, existing content library)

### Competitive Context
6. **Who are the top 3 competitors in content?** (May differ from product competitors)
7. **What topics do they own?** (Where are they ranking, what are they known for?)
8. **Where are the content gaps?** (Topics they haven't covered well or at all)

### Current State
9. **What content already exists?** (Blog posts, guides, videos, podcasts, newsletters)
10. **What's working?** (Top traffic pages, top converting content, most shared)
11. **What's not working?** (Content that gets no traffic, no engagement, no conversions)

## Step 2: Define Content Pillars

Content pillars are the 3-5 core themes your brand will be known for. Every piece of content should ladder up to a pillar.

### How to Choose Pillars
1. **Map your expertise.** What does your company know better than anyone else?
2. **Map your audience's problems.** What do they search for, ask about, struggle with?
3. **Find the overlap.** Pillars live at the intersection of your expertise and their problems.

### Pillar Criteria
A good pillar should be:
- **Broad enough** to support 20-50+ pieces of content.
- **Specific enough** that someone could say "Oh, they're the [pillar] company."
- **Connected to your product** — content should naturally lead to your solution.
- **Differentiated** — not the same pillars every competitor uses.

### Example Pillar Structure
For a project management tool:
| Pillar | Why It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| Remote team productivity | Matches audience pain, connects to product |
| Project planning frameworks | Search demand, demonstrates expertise |
| Cross-functional collaboration | Differentiator, real customer problem |
| Engineering team workflows | Persona-specific, high-intent |

### Anti-Patterns
- **Company news as a pillar.** Nobody searches for your product announcements.
- **Industry news as a pillar.** You'll never out-publish industry media. Offer analysis instead.
- **Too many pillars.** More than 5 means you haven't made strategic choices. Narrow down.

## Step 3: Topic Clusters

### Structure
Each pillar contains topic clusters. A cluster has:
1. **Pillar page** — a comprehensive, authoritative page on the broad topic (2,000-5,000 words). This is your main ranking target.
2. **Cluster content** — 8-15 supporting articles that cover subtopics in depth. Each links back to the pillar page.
3. **Internal linking** — cluster content links to the pillar page and to each other.

### Building a Cluster
1. Start with keyword research: what are people searching for within this topic?
2. Group keywords by subtopic and intent.
3. Assign one piece of content to each subtopic.
4. Map the internal linking structure before writing.

### Search Intent Categories
Every piece of content should target one intent type:
| Intent | What They Want | Content Type |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Informational | Learn about a topic | Blog post, guide, explainer |
| Navigational | Find a specific thing | Product page, comparison page |
| Commercial | Evaluate options | Comparison, review, "best of" list |
| Transactional | Take action | Landing page, pricing page, sign-up page |

### Keyword Prioritization
Prioritize topics that score high on at least two of these three:
1. **Search volume** — enough people search for this to be worth creating.
2. **Business relevance** — the topic naturally connects to your product.
3. **Rankability** — you can realistically rank for it given your domain authority and competition.

A topic with low volume but high business relevance and low competition is often a better investment than a high-volume, highly competitive keyword.

## Step 4: Content Types

### Blog Posts (600-1,500 words)
- **Best for:** Informational queries, building topical authority, regular publishing cadence.
- **Structure:** Hook, context, main content (sections with subheadings), takeaway, CTA.
- **Publishing cadence:** 2-4 per week for aggressive growth; 1-2 per week for steady growth; 2-4 per month for resource-constrained teams.

### Comprehensive Guides (2,000-5,000 words)
- **Best for:** Pillar pages, high-value search terms, lead magnets.
- **Structure:** Table of contents, introduction with scope, detailed sections, examples, summary, CTA.
- **Publishing cadence:** 1-2 per month.

### Case Studies
- **Best for:** Bottom-of-funnel, sales enablement, social proof.
- **Structure:** Customer context (who, industry, size), challenge (specific problem), solution (how they used your product), results (numbers), quote.
- **Key rule:** Lead with the result. "How [Company] increased revenue 40% in 6 months" not "How [Company] uses [Your Product]."
- **Publishing cadence:** 1-2 per month (depends on customer pipeline).

### Templates and Tools
- **Best for:** Lead generation, practical value, shareability.
- **Examples:** Spreadsheet templates, checklists, calculators, frameworks, swipe files.
- **Key rule:** The template should be genuinely useful on its own, not a thinly veiled product pitch.
- **Publishing cadence:** 1-2 per quarter.

### Comparison and Alternative Pages
- **Best for:** Commercial intent, capturing competitor traffic.
- **Structure:** Honest feature comparison, use-case fit, pricing comparison, verdict.
- **Key rule:** Be honest. Acknowledge where competitors are strong. Credibility matters more than spin.

### Thought Leadership
- **Best for:** Brand differentiation, earning trust, generating discussion.
- **Structure:** Contrarian take or unique insight, supporting evidence, implications.
- **Key rule:** Must contain an original perspective. Restating conventional wisdom is not thought leadership.

### Video and Audio
- **Best for:** Complex topics, product demos, interviews, building personal connection.
- **Key rule:** Always produce a written companion (transcript, summary, or derivative article) for SEO value.

## Step 5: Editorial Calendar

### Calendar Structure
| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Publish date | When it goes live |
| Content type | Blog, guide, case study, etc. |
| Title / topic | Working title and target keyword |
| Pillar | Which content pillar it serves |
| Funnel stage | Awareness, consideration, or decision |
| Author / owner | Who writes and who reviews |
| Status | Ideation, outline, draft, review, published |
| Distribution plan | Where and how it will be promoted |
| CTA | What the reader should do after reading |

### Planning Rhythm
- **Quarterly:** Set themes, major content initiatives, and pillar priorities.
- **Monthly:** Plan specific topics, assign authors, set deadlines.
- **Weekly:** Review pipeline status, address blockers, adjust priorities.

### Balancing the Calendar
Aim for a healthy mix across:
- **Funnel stages:** 60% top-of-funnel (awareness), 30% middle (consideration), 10% bottom (decision).
- **Content types:** Don't publish only blog posts. Rotate in guides, case studies, and templates.
- **Pillars:** Distribute content across all pillars each quarter. Don't let one pillar dominate.

### Content Batching
- Batch research: research 4-6 topics at once, then outline them all.
- Batch writing: dedicate full days to writing, not 30-minute blocks.
- Batch promotion: schedule distribution for a week's content in one sitting.

## Step 6: Distribution Channels

Creating content is half the job. Distribution is the other half. Most teams under-invest in distribution.

### Owned Channels
- **Blog / website:** Primary publishing platform. Optimize for SEO.
- **Email newsletter:** Curate and distribute content to subscribers. The most reliable channel.
- **Product / app:** Surface relevant content in-app (onboarding tips, feature guides, release notes).

### Social Channels
- **LinkedIn:** Best for B2B. Share insights, not just links. Add commentary.
- **Twitter/X:** Best for real-time, conversations, and thought leadership.
- **YouTube:** Best for tutorials, demos, and evergreen video content.
- **Others:** Choose based on where your audience actually spends time. Do not be on every platform.

### Earned Channels
- **SEO:** Optimize every piece for search. This is your compounding channel.
- **Guest posts:** Write for publications your audience reads. Quality over quantity.
- **Podcast appearances:** Be a guest on relevant podcasts. Prepare 2-3 talking points from your content.
- **Community participation:** Share expertise in Slack groups, forums, Reddit, and industry communities. Add value first, link second.

### Paid Amplification
- Boost top-performing organic content with paid social (LinkedIn Sponsored Content, Twitter Ads).
- Use retargeting to show content to website visitors who didn't convert.
- Sponsor relevant newsletters in your space.

### Distribution Checklist (Per Piece)
- [ ] Published on blog with proper SEO (title tag, meta description, internal links, images with alt text).
- [ ] Shared in email newsletter (or queued for next send).
- [ ] Posted on 1-2 social channels with native commentary (not just a link).
- [ ] Shared in relevant internal Slack channels for employee amplification.
- [ ] Submitted to relevant communities or forums (if appropriate and valuable).
- [ ] Repurposed into at least one other format (see Step 7).

## Step 7: Content Repurposing

Every piece of content should be repurposed into at least 2-3 derivative formats. This multiplies your output without multiplying your effort.

### Repurposing Chain
```
Long-form guide
  -> Blog post (excerpt or section as standalone)
  -> LinkedIn post (key insight + commentary)
  -> Twitter/X thread (step-by-step breakdown)
  -> Email newsletter feature
  -> Infographic or visual summary
  -> Short video (key points in 60-90 seconds)
  -> Slide deck (for webinars or presentations)
  -> Podcast episode discussion
```

### Repurposing Rules
1. **Adapt, don't just reformat.** A LinkedIn post is not the first paragraph of your blog post with a link. It's a native piece of content built from the same insight.
2. **Lead with the most interesting insight.** Not every section of a guide is worth repurposing. Pick the most provocative or useful parts.
3. **Match the format to the platform.** Threads on Twitter/X, carousels on LinkedIn, short-form on TikTok. Respect platform norms.
4. **Stagger timing.** Don't publish all derivative content on the same day. Spread it over 1-2 weeks.

## Step 8: Content Audit Framework

### When to Audit
- Quarterly for high-volume publishers (4+ pieces per week).
- Every 6 months for moderate publishers (1-3 per week).
- Annually at minimum.

### Audit Process
1. **Export all content** with performance data (traffic, rankings, conversions, engagement).
2. **Categorize each piece** into one of four buckets:

| Bucket | Criteria | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Keep | High traffic, good conversion, still accurate | Monitor, maintain |
| Update | Declining traffic, outdated info, or ranking on page 2 | Refresh content, update data, improve SEO |
| Consolidate | Multiple thin pieces on similar topics | Merge into one comprehensive piece |
| Remove | No traffic, no conversions, no strategic value | Redirect URL to relevant page, then delete |

3. **Prioritize updates** by potential impact: pages ranking positions 4-15 are your best candidates for quick wins.
4. **Check for cannibalization:** Are multiple pages competing for the same keyword? Consolidate.
5. **Verify all links, data, and examples** are current. Outdated content damages credibility.

### Content Refresh Checklist
- [ ] Update statistics and data points (anything older than 12 months).
- [ ] Add new sections covering developments since original publish date.
- [ ] Improve internal linking to/from newer content.
- [ ] Update the publish date (if substantially refreshed).
- [ ] Refresh meta title and description if click-through rate is low.
- [ ] Add or improve images, diagrams, and examples.

## Step 9: Measurement

### North Star Metrics by Goal
| Business Goal | Primary Metric | Supporting Metrics |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Brand awareness | Organic traffic | Branded search volume, social shares, backlinks |
| Lead generation | Marketing qualified leads from content | Email subscribers, form fills, content downloads |
| Sales enablement | Content influence on pipeline | Case study usage in deals, content shared by sales |
| Retention | Product adoption | Help doc usage, onboarding completion, NPS |

### Reporting Cadence
- **Weekly:** Traffic, top-performing content, new leads from content.
- **Monthly:** Keyword rankings, conversion rates, content pipeline health.
- **Quarterly:** Revenue attribution, content ROI, pillar performance, audit findings.

### Attribution
- Use UTM parameters consistently on every distributed link.
- Track first-touch and last-touch attribution separately. Content often influences at the top of funnel.
- Monitor assisted conversions — content that appears in the conversion path but isn't the last touch.

### What Not to Measure
- **Vanity metrics in isolation:** Page views without conversion data, social likes without engagement or traffic.
- **Content volume as a KPI:** Publishing 20 posts a month means nothing if none convert. Quality and strategic fit matter more than output.

## Output Format

When delivering a content strategy, include:

1. **Content pillars** — 3-5 pillars with rationale and example topics.
2. **Topic cluster map** — for at least one pillar, show the full cluster (pillar page + supporting content).
3. **Editorial calendar** — 4-8 weeks of planned content with titles, types, pillars, owners, and dates.
4. **Distribution plan** — channel strategy and per-piece distribution checklist.
5. **Measurement framework** — KPIs, reporting cadence, and tools.
6. **Quick wins** — 3-5 content pieces to create first, based on highest potential impact.
7. **Assumptions and questions** — what you assumed and what needs the user's input.
